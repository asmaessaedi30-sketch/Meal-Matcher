💡 How to Read Your Workout Guide

⏱️ Tempo (e.g., 2-0-2-0 or 1-0-1-0)

The speed (in seconds) for each phase of a single repetition:

  • 1st number: Time spent lowering the weight (e.g., squatting down).
  • 2nd number: Pause at the bottom of the movement.
  • 3rd number: Time spent lifting the weight (e.g., standing back up).
  • 4th number: Pause at the top.

Example: 2-0-2-0 means lower for 2s, no pause, lift for 2s, no pause. 1-0-1-0 is a standard steady pace.

🔥 Effort / RPE (Rate of Perceived Exertion)

A difficulty scale from 1 to 10 measuring how hard the exercise feels to you:

  • RPE 10: Maximum effort (you cannot do even one more repetition).
  • RPE 8: Very hard (you feel tired but could do 2 more reps if pushed).
  • RPE 7: Moderately hard (you could do 3 more reps).
  • RPE 1-4: Warm-up or light stretching intensity.
💤 Rest Intervals

The duration (e.g., 60s) you should rest and catch your breath between sets before starting your next set of repetitions.
